Common Questions

What is "Dual Enrollment"?

Dual enrollment allows high school students to take college-level courses and simultaneously earn both high school and college credit for that course. It's a fantastic way to get a head start on your college education.

Who is eligible to take this course?

This course is primarily designed for high school freshmen and sophomores who will be juniors or seniors by the time they take the course. Students should have a strong interest in science, medicine, research, or STEM fields. Specific GPA requirements may apply; please check with your high school counselor.

What are the prerequisites for this course?

While there are no strict scientific prerequisites beyond a general interest in science, a strong foundation in high school biology and chemistry is recommended.

How many college credits will I earn?

The "Introduction to Biotechnology Lab" course in total awards 3 college credits. These credits are transferable to UC and CSU; however, we recommend checking with institutions that aren't a UC or CSU if you plan to apply to them.

What kind of hands-on activities will I do in the lab?

You'll gain practical experience in core biotech techniques, including but not limited to: lab safety and notebook keeping, micropipetting, pH measurement, microscopy, spectrophotometry, gel electrophoresis, ELISA, bacterial transformation, and potentially DNA barcoding. See our Lab Skills section for more details on certain areas!

What are the potential career benefits of taking this course?

This course provides foundational skills highly valued in fields such as medical diagnostics, pharmaceutical research, forensic science, environmental biotechnology, and agricultural science. It prepares you for college-level science and helps you explore potential STEM career pathways. Visit our Career Pathways page for more information.

Is there a cost for this course?

Costs for dual enrollment are free between El Modena and Santiago Canyon College. The dual enrollment programs offer courses free of charge for high school students.

How do I apply?

The first step is to go to college and career center to confirm eligibility and discuss the dual enrollment process. They will guide you through obtaining necessary permissions and any college application steps. You can also find more details on our Enrollment page.

What if I'm not sure if science is for me?

This course is an excellent opportunity to explore your interest in science in a practical, engaging way. The hands-on nature of the lab might just spark a passion you didn't know you had! It's a low-risk way to experience college-level science before you commit to a major.
